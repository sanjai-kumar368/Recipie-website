Sweet Rasgulla Recipe

Rasgulla is a classic Bengali dessert made from fresh chenna (curdled milk) balls cooked in light sugar syrup until spongy and juicy. It’s soft, melt-in-the-mouth, and soaked in sweetness!

Ingredients

• 1 litre Full Cream Milk
• 2 tbsp Lemon Juice (or Vinegar) diluted in 2 tbsp water
• 1 cup Sugar
• 4 cups Water
• 2–3 Cardamom Pods
• A few drops of Rose Water (optional)

Making Process

  1. Boil milk and curdle it by adding diluted lemon juice. Once chenna separates, strain it using a muslin cloth and rinse under water.
  2. Squeeze out excess water and hang the cloth for 30 mins. Knead the chenna into a soft, smooth dough for 7–8 minutes.
  3. Make small, crack-free balls and cover them.
  4. Boil 4 cups water with sugar and cardamom. Once boiling, gently add rasgulla balls, cover with lid and cook on high for 10–12 minutes.
  5. They will double in size. Add rose water at the end and let them cool in the syrup.
  6. Chill before serving for best taste!
