Idli Recipe

Idli is a soft, steamed, and fluffy South Indian delicacy made from fermented rice and urad dal (black gram) batter. It is a staple breakfast in many Indian households and is known for its health benefits and light texture.

Ingredients for Idli

- 1 cup Urad Dal (black gram)
- 2 cups Parboiled Rice
- 1/4 tsp Fenugreek Seeds
- Salt to taste
- Water as needed
- Oil for greasing idli molds

How to Make Idli

  1. Rinse urad dal and fenugreek seeds. Soak them together in water for 4–6 hours.
  2. Rinse and soak rice separately for the same duration.
  3. Grind the urad dal to a smooth and fluffy batter using little water. Transfer to a large bowl.
  4. Grind the rice to a slightly coarse batter and mix both together.
  5. Add salt and mix well. Cover and allow it to ferment overnight (8–12 hours).
  6. Once fermented, gently mix the batter.
  7. Grease idli molds, pour batter into them and steam for 10–12 minutes.
  8. Check with a toothpick — if it comes out clean, your idlis are done.
  9. Let them cool for a minute, then remove using a spoon. Serve hot with chutney.

Coconut Chutney

This creamy and refreshing chutney is a perfect side for idli, dosa, vada and more!

Ingredients for Chutney

- 1 cup grated Coconut (fresh or frozen)
- 2 tbsp Roasted Chana Dal (pottukadalai)
- 1 Green Chili (adjust to spice level)
- 1 small piece of Ginger
- Salt to taste
- Water as required

Tempering (Optional but Flavorful)

- 1 tsp Oil
- 1/2 tsp Mustard Seeds
- A few Curry Leaves
- 1 Dry Red Chili

How to Make Coconut Chutney

  1. Add coconut, chana dal, green chili, ginger, salt, and water to a blender. Blend until smooth.
  2. Transfer to a bowl. Adjust water for desired consistency.
  3. Heat oil in a small pan, add mustard seeds. Let them splutter.
  4. Add red chili and curry leaves. Pour this tempering over the chutney.
  5. Mix and serve with hot idlis!
